About Weekly Waste

Why am I sharing my weekly trash with a bunch of random strangers on the internet?

Well, I wasn’t happy with the number of trash bags I was going through and knew I didn’t want to continue living the way I was. In an effort to reduce my own waste, I decided to start posting online some of the things I got rid of each week for the world to see, to encourage me to pick up more eco-friendly habits. Intending for the experiment to only last a few weeks, I was shocked to see just how many people would end up viewing my page and so I made the decision to continue the project to this day.

Before starting this project, I never really thought about what or how much trash I threw away on a regular basis. Each week that I do this, I get more insights into my overall habits which inspire me to make changes in my life. On top of that, I didn’t realize how many memories and stories could be contained in a series of inanimate objects destined for the bin. Compiling each post gives me the opportunity to recap the events of the past week, something I wouldn’t really get the chance to do otherwise.

Now I can’t take full credit for coming up with the idea of recording one’s trash each week because I was inspired by a few others:

Sarah Newman, an archaeologist who studies the trash of ancient societies, teaches about the many different aspects of life that we can learn from looking at the things people toss out. Through her work, she has also come to understand just how easy it is to justify our excessive waste because everyone else, past and present, does the same.

Vik Muniz is a contemporary artist known for his use of unconventional materials, including trash, in his art. In his “Pictures of Garbage” series, he used the garbage from a landfill in Brazil to create portraits of the people who lived and worked there. Seeing this collection, as well as his other work, I realized just how many stories could be told from the things that people throw out and no longer care about.

Robin Greenfield, an environmental activist, took on a challenge to bring awareness to the amount of trash an individual can accumulate in a short amount of time. Over the course of 30 days, he lived like a normal person, but with one big difference. Instead of throw out his garbage, he decided to wear it! The amount of trash he was wearing by the end of the challenge was more than I expected, helping me to think about just how much trash I throw out and what I can do to reduce it.
